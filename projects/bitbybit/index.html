WORK STATION

  • Before we start building, we'll want to prepare our work area with:
    • A clutter free table
    • An anti static surface
    • Tools: screwdriver, box cutter, needle nose pliers, thermal compound, cable ties
    • Optional Items: container for screws, side cutters, anti-static strap
  • Setting up an Anti Static Work Station
  • In order to avoid ruining your computer components with static electricity while working on it, you'll want to:
    1. Avoid rubbing your socks against carpeted floors.
    2. Plug in your power supply before working, leave it off, and before you touch a piece of your computer you touch the power supply to ground yourself. You can also purchase an anti-static strap and connect it to the power supply in order to avoid having to remember to reground yourself.

STEP 1

  1. Grab the motherboard and put the IO shield to the side.
  2. Use the motherboard box as a work station and take the CPU out of it's box.
  3. Push down the lever on the CPU holder, pull out, and push up to move the retention plate out of the way.
  4. Line up the triangle or alternate marking that is on the corner of the CPU with the marking on the corner of the CPU slot.
  5. Place the CPU on top of the pins in the proper orientation and let it drop into place, giving it a wiggle to make sure it's secure.
  6. Lower the lever on the side and put it back into place. Remove the plastic shield covering the CPU and place it back in the box.

STEP 2

  1. Next we have the RAM. Get both sticks out of their packing.
  2. Find the RAM slots on the top right of the motherboard. You'll want to leave an empty slot between each stick of RAM.
  3. Push back one of the levers on the end of the RAM slots and line up the notch on the bottom of the stick with the notch in the slot.
  4. Apply even pressure on both sides of the stick of RAM and push down until you hear a click on each end.

  5. If you have an m.2 SSD now is the time to install that.
    • Find the m.2 slot on the motherboard. Some may be on the back or under heatspreaders or shrouds.
    • Slide the flat end into the slot and gently hold in place while you it screw in.

STEP 3

  1. Now for the CPU cooler. Installation for this item will vary depending on the model, but the basic instructions are the same.
  2. Unscrew the brackets from the front and get the backplate for the cooler from the box.
  3. Turn the motherboard over and place the backplate on. When handling the motherboard, try to only touch the edges or metal components, to avoid getting skin oils on the PCB (Printed Circuit Board).
  4. Grab the thermal compound. You'll want to apply a very small amount to the CPU to provide a thin layer once squished down by the cooler. You can always wipe it off if there is too much and try again.
  5. Screw in the cooler and connect the 4 pin to the cpu_fan1 slot above the CPU.
